About

Daniel F. Corrin

Mediator & Arbitrator

Daniel Corrin is a highly experienced personal injury lawyer with more than 20 years of legal experience in complex injury and dispute resolution matters. His practice is grounded in a deep understanding of both the legal system and the human impact of serious conflict, allowing him to guide parties toward thoughtful, practical, and lasting resolutions.

Before transitioning to dispute resolution as a mediator and arbitrator, Daniel was a principal lawyer at Parsons Corrin LLP, where he built a distinguished career in catastrophic injury litigation. He has extensive trial experience involving traumatic brain injuries, spinal cord injuries, medical malpractice, and complex motor vehicle claims—giving him firsthand insight into litigation risk and the cost of prolonged disputes.

Daniel’s mediation and arbitration work is informed by decades of engagement with the brain injury, legal, and medical communities. He has served on boards, led continuing legal education programs, and volunteered with advisory groups across British Columbia.

As a mediator and arbitrator, Daniel brings a calm, structured, and empathetic approach to dispute resolution. He is recognized for listening carefully, understanding the interests of all parties, and helping participants move beyond entrenched positions toward workable outcomes.
